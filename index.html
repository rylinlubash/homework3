About Me

My name is Rylin Lubash. I'm a senior at Northeastern University studying Behavioral Neuroscience with a minor in Data Science. I’m originally from Washington state, right outside of Seattle. In May, I’ll be graduating and am hoping to continue my career in neuroscience research and eventually pursue a PhD in Neuroscience. I currently volunteer in a lab on campus where we study the sex differences in fear responses and the underlying circuitry in rats using Pavlovian fear conditioning and chemogenetics. The plan is to stay in the general field of learning and memory and fear in my next research position, which I am currently on the hunt for.



Outside of my work and academics, I enjoy baking and reading. It can be hard to find time for hobbies during the busy semester, but I do my best. Lately, I’ve been reading Babel by R.F. Kuang, which is a fascinating fantasy novel full of meticulous worldbuilding to make you believe in their magic. I also read The Mountain Under the Sea by Ray Nayler a few months ago, and it was definitely my top book of 2024. It’s about a future where artificial intelligence and robots run rampant in a Earth stricken by climate change, and a newly discovered species of octopus that has developed a language.
